## What Is PikaPods?

PikaPods deploys each application in its own isolated container (a "Pod") and handles everything underneath: server provisioning, software updates, HTTPS certificates, firewalling, and container isolation. Pick an app, choose a region, set your resources, and click Add Pod. Within minutes your app is live — no SSH, no Docker, no server management required.

### Key Features

- **Fully managed updates.** App updates are tested and applied weekly — no maintenance burden on you.
- **No tracking, no ads.** PikaPods sells hosting, not data.

## The Three Apps

### Nextcloud — Private File Storage & Collaboration

Nextcloud replaces Dropbox, Google Drive, and Google Workspace in one self-hosted pod. Beyond file sync, it includes calendar and contacts syncing, collaborative document editing (via Collabora or OnlyOffice), video calls, and project boards — all with no per-user seat fees.

**Key setup steps**

- **Custom domain.** Configure your domain in Nextcloud's admin panel to avoid browser warnings.
- **External storage.** Connect an S3-compatible bucket (Backblaze B2, Wasabi) for large file libraries.
- **Desktop & mobile clients.** Official clients for Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, and Android sync files just like Dropbox.

> Dropbox Business starts at $15/user/month. A Nextcloud pod covering the same team costs roughly $40–50/year.

### WordPress — The World's Most Flexible Website Platform

WordPress powers over 40% of all websites. WordPress.com's Business plan starts at $25/month and adds restrictions on plugins and themes. A PikaPods WordPress pod starts at ~$3–4/month with full admin access, unlimited plugins, and no artificial feature limits.

**Key setup steps**

- **Custom domain.** Point DNS to PikaPods and update the Site URL in WordPress General Settings.
- **Caching plugin.** W3 Total Cache or WP Super Cache improves performance significantly on smaller pods.
- **Transactional email.** PikaPods doesn't include outbound email. Connect a free Mailgun or Brevo account for forms and WooCommerce.
- **Backups.** Use UpdraftPlus to auto-backup your database and files to an S3 bucket or Google Drive.

### Ghost — Modern Publishing for Newsletters & Blogs

Ghost is the platform of choice for newsletters, blogs, and content-driven businesses — a compelling alternative to Substack and Medium where you own your subscriber list. Ghost Pro's Creator plan runs $25/month ($300/year). Ghost on PikaPods costs ~$2–3/month with no feature restrictions, full theme customization, and your own custom domain.

**Key setup steps**

- **Custom domain.** Update Settings > Publication in the Ghost admin panel, then point your DNS.
- **Mailgun for email.** Ghost requires an external SMTP provider for newsletters. Mailgun's free tier handles low-volume sending.
- **Members & newsletters.** Enable Ghost's built-in membership system and connect Stripe for paid subscriber tiers.

> Crucially, Ghost on PikaPods costs the same $2–3/month whether you have 500 or 50,000 subscribers. Ghost Pro pricing scales with your membership count — PikaPods pricing scales with server resources, which grow far more slowly.

### Zero Server Administration

The classic objection to self-hosting is maintenance burden. PikaPods eliminates this entirely. Updates are automatically applied weekly. HTTPS certificates auto-renew. You interact with your apps as an administrator, never as a system administrator.

This is the key distinction from a VPS like DigitalOcean: a Droplet gives more control, but you also maintain the server. PikaPods trades low-level control for a completely maintenance-free experience.

### No Per-Seat Pricing

SaaS products charge per user — every hire increases monthly spend. PikaPods pricing is based on computing resources, not headcount. A Nextcloud pod serving 5 users costs the same as one serving 50, as long as the resource footprint stays similar. This asymmetry compounds significantly as teams grow.

### Data Ownership & No Vendor Lock-In

When your business uses WordPress.com, Ghost Pro, or Dropbox, your content lives under their terms of service. Platforms change pricing, discontinue features, and get acquired. With PikaPods, your data is yours — accessible via SFTP at any time, in open formats, ready to move to any other host. Ghost content exports cleanly as JSON; WordPress exports full XML; Nextcloud files are standard folders.

### Support for Open Source

PikaPods shares 20% of pod revenue with original open source project authors. By hosting Nextcloud, WordPress, or Ghost on PikaPods, you directly fund the development of the software your business depends on — something a SaaS subscription never does.
